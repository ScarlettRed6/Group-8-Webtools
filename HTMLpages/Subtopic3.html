DON'T GET CLICK BAITED

Anyone who has used the internet may have come across the term “clickbait” where it refers to content that is meant to provoke user interest through eye-catching thumbnails, images, titles, or descriptions that urges them to click on the misleading content. While it may seem like you are getting the intriguing information you are looking for by clicking on the “clickbait” content, you are only being tricked into watching less-interesting content, or content that has nothing to do with those mentioned in the thumbnails and titles. It is of utmost importance that we scrutinize these clickbait materials in order for us to obtain truthful information. Clickbaits rely on attention-grabbing tactics that take advantage of human curiosity, emotions, and desires. It uses exaggerated, takes advantage of current trends, or simply using misleading information to lure users for engagement. More often than not, the content and information under clickbaits rarely live up to the user’s expectation, which leads to frustration and disappointment for the user. Clickbaits can be found in a multitude of online platforms, from social media websites, to news outlets and video-sharing websites. These headlines make use of attention-grabbing details that would exploit human curiosities towards shocking or unbelievable information, which leads users towards content that does not match their expectations. Clickbait content often leads to disappointment, frustration, and wasted time, providing unsuspecting users with misleading information that fails to meet their expectations. By exercising caution, keeping your guard up, and carefully scrutinizing information sources, online users can navigate the massive cyberspace more effectively, reducing the chances of being subject to the manipulative tactics of clickbait content.
