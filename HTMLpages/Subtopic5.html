PHISHING ATTACK

                What is Phishing Attack 

                Phishing attacks is the act of sending a deceptive communication
                that appears convincing and legitimate and looks like it came from 
                a reputable source. This is usually recieved through emails and text
                messages. The attacker's goal is to gain your login credentials, 
                steal money, and install malware on your accounts. 

                Who is the Victim of Phishing

                Phishing, This type of attacks does not choose its victim, everyone
                is a prey for phishing attack. The attackers sends numerous emails 
                hoping that all of the reciever would click the link that is sent to 
                start their malicious activities,  steal money and your credentials.

                How does phishing work?

                Phishing is the most effective attack as it exploits the vulnerability
                of human nature, as we humans are curious about things, this is the 
                reason that may lead us to clicking everything we see on social media 
                even if this may not look like a trusted link. Phishing happens when a 
                person is lured by a legitimate-looking email or text message from a 
                trusted sender and when the message is clicked it may be a website that 
                looks legitimate and once you're on the website, it will automatically 
                download malware and ransomware. It only takes your one click/tap for the attacker 
                to steal important informations about you and your account that's why it's 
                important to Think Before You Click.

                Here are some examples of Phishing scenario;

                - An email is sent to you saying that you can win a phone in a raffle and you just need to enter your email account and password. 
                - An urgent text message is sent to you saying that your password is expiring within 24 hours.
                
                Potential consequences

                - If you enter you login credentials, the attacker will gain access to your account.
                - The attacker may use your account for malicious activities.

                How to recognize phishing attack

                Phishing attack is a dangerous type of data breaching, 
                therefore recognizing how it looks and its characteristics 
                is one of the way to avoid being a victim of this data breach. 
                Here is how you van recognize Phishing attack;

                Look at the address not the name
                - always check the sender's address because this 
                is where you can determine what is the IP address where the email is sent.

                Strange attachments or links
                - always double check links and attachments.

                Sense of Urgency
                - Phishing wants you to input your login credentials immediately to get your credentials fast.

                Analyze the salutation
                -  Phishing messages always start in a salutation, for example "Dear Employee".
