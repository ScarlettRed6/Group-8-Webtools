What is a MALWARE

"Malware" is a portmanteau of the words "malicious" and "software." It is a broad term used to describe any type of software specifically 
designed to harm, exploit, or otherwise compromise the integrity of a computer system, network, or user's data. 
 Malware encompasses a wide range of malicious programs, each with its own set of functions and objectives. 
Common types of malware include viruses, worms, trojan horses, ransomware, spyware, adware, and more.
Malware can be distributed through various means, including email attachments, malicious websites, infected software downloads, and removable storage devices. 
Once it infiltrates a system, malware may execute malicious actions without the user's knowledge or consent, 
such as stealing sensitive information, disrupting system operations, or providing unauthorized access to a remote attacker.
Protection against malware involves using security measures such as antivirus and anti-malware software, keeping operating systems and software up to date, 
practicing safe browsing habits, being cautious with email attachments and links, and regularly backing up important data. 
The landscape of malware is dynamic, with new threats emerging regularly, making it crucial for users and organizations to stay vigilant and employ security best practices.

Common Malwares to be aware of:

                Computer Viruses: These are malicious programs that attach themselves to legitimate executable files and spread when these files are executed.

                Worms: Worms are self-replicating malware that can spread independently without human intervention. They often exploit vulnerabilities in network services.
                
                Trojan Horses: Trojans disguise themselves as legitimate software but contain malicious code. They can create backdoors, steal data, or perform other harmful actions.
                
                Ransomware: Ransomware encrypts a user's files and demands payment (usually in cryptocurrency) for the decryption key. It's a type of malware that can cause significant data loss and financial damage.
                
                Spyware: Spyware is designed to spy on the user's activities. It can capture keystrokes, take screenshots, and collect sensitive information.
                
                Adware: Adware displays unwanted advertisements to users. While not as malicious as some other types of malware, it can be annoying and may compromise privacy.
                
                Phishing Attacks: While not a specific type of malware, phishing involves tricking users into providing sensitive information, such as usernames, passwords, or credit card numbers, often through deceptive emails or websites.
                
                Drive-by Downloads: Users can unwittingly download malware when visiting compromised websites. The malware is typically downloaded and executed without the user's knowledge.
                
                Botnets: Botnets are networks of compromised computers controlled by a single entity. They can be used for various malicious purposes, including distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks.
                
                Keyloggers: Keyloggers record keystrokes on a computer, allowing attackers to capture sensitive information like usernames and passwords.
